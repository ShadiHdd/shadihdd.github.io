About

My doctoral research integrates control theory, optimization, machine learning, and set-based reachability analysis. The central approach in this work leverages geometric properties of specific classes of systems in order to produce a new subgenre of theory and algorithms for set estimation. In particular, my dissertation provides a collection of novel results and algorithms for estimating reach sets: Computing such sets is critical for guaranteeing the performance of machine learning and decision-making pipelines across safety-critical applications such as robotics, automated driving, and medical prognostics.

Work & Research

Graduate Student Researcher with the University of California, Santa Cruz (2019-2024)

As a researcher in the Applied Mathematics Department at the University of California, Santa Cruz, I create and implement analytical and numerical methods essential for understanding the dynamics of control systems at the forefront of emerging technologies. Specifically, my responsibilities include proposing original research questions, developing theoretical result,implementing numerical experiments and computational simulations, writing research papers, and presenting our findings at top conferences.
