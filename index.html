about

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in Applied Mathematics at the University of California, Santa Cruz. My work integrates control theory, optimization, machine learning (ML) and set-based reachability analysis. Specifically, I am currently designing novel neural architectures and optimization algorithms to learn reachable sets subject to controlled dynamics and set-valued uncertainties. These sets capture the collection of states a dynamical agent may occupy at a future time based on imperfect knowledge of the dynamics and uncertainties. Computing such sets is critical for guaranteeing the performance of the ML prediction and decision-making pipeline across safety-critical applications, including robotics, automated driving, and medical prognostics.
